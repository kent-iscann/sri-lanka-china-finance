# Tech Pivot

Kazakhstan's attempt to diversify its economy through technology — AI, cryptocurrency mining, and startup ecosystem development.

## AI ambitions
- **Ministry of Artificial Intelligence and Digital Development:** Created to lead national AI strategy.
- **Astana Hub:** Government-backed tech park and startup incubator in the capital.
- **AI & Digital Bridge 2026:** Major international IT forum scheduled for October 2026, focused on AI and digital transformation.
- **KPMG AI Readiness Report (2025):** Assessed Kazakhstan's AI ecosystem, identifying both progress and significant gaps.
- **Dealroom.co ranking:** Kazakhstan entered the top 10 "Rising Stars" in the Global Tech Ecosystem Index 2026.

## Cryptocurrency mining
- After China banned crypto mining in 2021, Kazakhstan became one of the world's largest Bitcoin mining hubs.
- Mining operations strained the power grid, causing blackouts and public protests in 2022.
- Government partially banned crypto mining in 2024 to manage electricity demand.
- Some mining operations have pivoted to AI data center services.

## Startup ecosystem
- Astana Hub serves as the primary startup incubator, offering tax incentives and co-working spaces.
- Fintech, enterprise AI, and healthtech are the main focus areas.
- The ecosystem is small (~89 companies on F6S) and heavily dependent on government support.
- Limited private venture capital. Most funding comes from state-linked sources.

## Assessment
The tech pivot is real but nascent. Kazakhstan has made progress in creating the institutional framework (ministry, hub, forums) and attracting attention (GITEX, Dealroom ranking). However, the ecosystem remains small, government-dependent, and faces structural challenges including brain drain, corruption, and competition from more established tech hubs. The authoritarian governance model and limited internet freedom also constrain the open innovation culture that drives successful tech ecosystems.
