# Caspian Geopolitics

The Caspian Sea is a critical arena for Kazakhstan's energy transit, regional competition, and great power dynamics.

## Strategic significance
- **Energy transit:** The Caspian basin contains significant oil and gas reserves. Kazakhstan's major oil fields (Tengiz, Kashagan) are on or near the Caspian coast.
- **Transport corridors:** The North-South transport route across the Caspian connects Russia to Iran and the Middle East. Kazakhstan is a key node.
- **Pipeline politics:** The CPC pipeline (Caspian Pipeline Consortium) carries Kazakh oil to the Russian Black Sea port of Novorossiysk. Alternative routes (through Azerbaijan, Georgia) are being developed but remain limited.
- **2026 production downgrade:** Kazakhstan revised its 2026 oil production target down to 98 million tons (from 100.5M) due to Tengiz field fires/equipment outages and CPC pipeline attacks/infrastructure issues. The CPC remains a critical bottleneck.

## Sanctions dimension
- Russia uses Caspian littoral states (including Kazakhstan) to re-route trade and energy flows to bypass Western sanctions.
- The Caspian has become a "gray zone" for sanctions evasion, with ship-to-ship transfers and transshipment through Kazakhstan.
- This creates significant secondary sanctions risk for Kazakhstan.
- **EU 20th sanctions package (April 2026):** For the first time, the EU deployed a tool to block supplies of sanctioned goods to third countries systematically used for circumvention. Kyrgyzstan was the first target. Kazakhstan faces growing risk as an EAEU member with documented sanctions-evasion flows.

## Regional competition
- **Russia:** Wants to maintain dominance over Caspian energy transit and use it as a sanctions-bypass corridor.
- **Iran:** Competes for Caspian energy market share and transit routes.
- **Azerbaijan:** Developing alternative energy routes (BTC pipeline, TANAP) that bypass both Russia and Iran.
- **Turkmenistan:** Competes for gas market share and pipeline access.
- **Turkey:** TPAO's entry into Kazakhstan's energy market (2026) signals Ankara's push for a permanent institutional role in Central Asia's energy equation.

## Middle Corridor
- The Trans-Caspian International Transport Route (Middle Corridor) is being framed not just as a trade route but as an energy corridor to carry Kazakh resources to the West.
- Kazakhstan suspended several joint projects with Iran in April 2026 and placed greater emphasis on the Middle Corridor, which bypasses both Russia and vulnerable southern routes.
- Turkey-Kazakhstan energy ties deepening: Kazakhstan is Turkey's leading trade partner in the Turkic world as of 2025.

## Key question
Whether Kazakhstan can use its Caspian position as leverage in great power competition, or whether it will be pulled into Russia's sanctions-era architecture.
