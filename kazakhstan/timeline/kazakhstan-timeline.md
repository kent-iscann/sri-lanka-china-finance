# Timeline: Kazakhstan — Global Competitiveness

## 1991
- **December:** Kazakhstan declares independence from the Soviet Union. Nursultan Nazarbayev becomes president.

## 1993–2000s
- Kazakhstan opens its oil and gas sector to foreign investment. Tengiz, Kashagan, and Karachaganak mega-fields attract Western majors (Chevron, ExxonMobil, Shell).
- Economy becomes heavily hydrocarbon-dependent. Oil and gas account for ~60% of exports and ~40% of budget revenue.

## 2003
- China and Kazakhstan sign early Belt and Road precursor agreements for pipeline and infrastructure development.

## 2008–2009
- Global financial crisis hits Kazakhstan's commodity-dependent economy hard. Government launches anti-crisis program.
- Creation of Samruk-Kazyna sovereign wealth fund to consolidate state assets.

## 2010
- Kazakhstan chairs the OSCE, signaling its ambition to be recognized as a serious international actor.

## 2013
- China announces the Belt and Road Initiative. Kazakhstan becomes a key transit corridor for China-Europe trade.

## 2014–2015
- Oil prices collapse. Kazakhstan's economy enters recession. Tenge devalued by ~40%.
- Government announces "100 Concrete Steps" institutional reform program.

## 2017
- Government targets a shift to renewable energy. Blockchain and fintech initiatives announced.
- Astana (renamed Nur-Sultan) hosts EXPO 2017 on the theme "Future Energy."

## 2019
- **June:** Kassym-Jomart Tokayev succeeds Nazarbayev as president. Nazarbayev retains significant influence as Chairman of the Security Council and "Leader of the Nation."

## 2020
- COVID-19 pandemic disrupts economy. Oil prices briefly go negative.
- Government launches digitalization push, including e-governance platforms.

## 2022
- **January:** Bloody protests in Almaty and across the country triggered by fuel price hikes. Tokayev orders crackdown, requests CSTO intervention.
- Russia's invasion of Ukraine reshapes Kazakhstan's strategic calculus. Tokayev publicly distances from Moscow on territorial integrity.
- Kazakhstan begins tightening compliance with Western sanctions against Russia while maintaining deep economic ties.
- Cryptocurrency mining surges as Chinese miners relocate to Kazakhstan, straining the power grid. Blackouts and protests follow.

## 2023
- Tokayev consolidates power, removes Nazarbyev from key positions.
- Government announces AI and digital transformation strategy.
- Kazakhstan applies to host major international tech events.

## 2024
- Partial ban on cryptocurrency mining implemented after grid instability and public backlash.
- KPMG publishes Kazakhstan AI Readiness Report.
- EU–Central Asia Summit in Samarkand signals growing European interest in reducing dependence on Russian energy routes.

## 2025
- Kazakhstan enters the top 10 of Dealroom.co's Global Tech Ecosystem Index "Rising Stars" category.
- EU increases engagement with Central Asia after Ukraine war shows need for diversified energy sources.
- Russia increases pressure on Kazakhstan to serve as a sanctions-bypass corridor for trade and energy flows.
- Putin-Tokayev meetings (May) highlight the push-pull dynamic: Russia anchoring, Kazakhstan leveraging.

## 2026
- **April:** Data Center & AI Infrastructure Central Asia forum held in Astana.
- **May:** Putin visits Kazakhstan; both sides discuss energy, logistics, nuclear cooperation.
- **June:** AI & Digital Bridge 2026 forum announced for October, organized by the Ministry of Artificial AI and Digital Development. UK lowers Russian oil price cap, increasing secondary sanctions risk for Kazakhstan.
