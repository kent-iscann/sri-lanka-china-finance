# Nursultan Nazarbayev

First President of Kazakhstan (1991–2019), now "Leader of the Nation" (Elbasy). Despite formally stepping down in 2019, Nazarbayev retained significant influence until the January 2022 protests, after which Tokayev stripped him of key positions.

## Legacy
- Built Kazakhstan's oil and gas economy through partnerships with Western majors
- Maintained multi-vector foreign policy balancing Russia, China, and the West
- Created the institutional framework (Samruk-Kazyna, Astana Hub) that underpins current diversification efforts
- Authoritarian governance model with limited political freedoms

## Continuing influence
- The "Nazarbayer era" institutional legacy shapes all current policy. The ruling party, state-owned enterprises, and security structures remain largely intact.
- His removal from power in 2022 was a watershed moment but did not fundamentally alter the system he built.
