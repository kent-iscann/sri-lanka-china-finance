# Kassym-Jomart Tokayev

President of Kazakhstan since June 2019, succeeding Nursultan Nazarbayev. Tokayev has pursued a reform agenda while maintaining the authoritarian structures of his predecessor.

## Key positions
- **President of Kazakhstan** (2019–present)
- **Chairman of the Security Council** (2022–present, after removing Nazarbayev)
- **Chairman of the ruling party Amanat** (2022–present)

## Political trajectory
- **2019–2022:** Initially seen as a continuation of Nazarbayev's rule. Consolidated power after the January 2022 protests by removing Nazarbayev from key positions.
- **2022–present:** Publicly distanced from Russia's invasion of Ukraine while maintaining deep economic ties. Pursued multi-vector foreign policy with increased engagement with EU, China, and Gulf states.
- **2025–2026:** Facing the challenge of balancing Russia's sanctions-era pressure with Western engagement and domestic reform demands.

## Key policies
- Institutional reform ("100 Concrete Steps")
- Digital transformation and AI strategy
- Multi-vector foreign policy diversification
- Tightening sanctions compliance while preserving Russian economic ties
