# China

China is Kazakhstan's second-largest trading partner and a key source of investment, particularly in energy, mining, and infrastructure.

## Key dimensions
- **Belt and Road Initiative:** Kazakhstan is a critical transit corridor for China-Europe rail freight. The Khorgos Gateway dry port on the China-Kazakhstan border is a flagship BRI project.
- **Trade:** China-Kazakhstan trade reached $227.9 billion in 2025. China imports oil, gas, uranium, and minerals from Kazakhstan.
- **Investment:** Chinese companies have invested heavily in Kazakhstan's energy sector, including the Kashagan oil field and various pipeline projects.
- **Cryptocurrency mining:** After China banned crypto mining in 2021, many miners relocated to Kazakhstan, straining the power grid and contributing to the 2022 blackouts.

## Current dynamic
China's economic influence in Kazakhstan is growing but remains secondary to Russia's in terms of security and institutional ties. Kazakhstan views China as a counterweight to Russian dominance, but is cautious about over-dependence on Beijing.
