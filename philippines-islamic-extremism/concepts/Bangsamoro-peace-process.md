# Bangsamoro Peace Process

The ongoing implementation of the 2014 Comprehensive Agreement on the Bangsamoro (CAB) between the MILF and the Philippine government.

## Status
- BARMM established in 2019 replacing ARMM
- Transitional government (BTA — Bangsamoro Transition Authority) led by MILF
- First parliamentary elections: September 14, 2026 — the critical near-term milestone
- Government targets exit agreement by 2028 (before Marcos leaves office)

## Risk factors
- **Spoiler groups:** MILF hardliners, Abu Sayyaf remnants, and IS-affiliated cells benefit from peace process failure
- **Manila interference:** Marcos administration's oversight posture risks alienating MILF leadership
- **Election violence:** September 2026 elections could trigger clan-based (rido) violence that extremists exploit
- **Resource control:** Disputes over Bangsamoro's fiscal autonomy and natural resource revenue sharing with Manila

## Assessment
The peace process is at its most fragile point since 2019. Successful elections would consolidate the MILF's political legitimacy and undermine extremist recruitment narratives. Failed or fraudulent elections could reverse years of progress.
