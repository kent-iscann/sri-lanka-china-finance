# Bangsamoro Peace Process

The ongoing implementation of the 2014 Comprehensive Agreement on the Bangsamoro (CAB) between the MILF and the Philippine government.

## Status
- BARMM established in 2019 replacing ARMM
- Transitional government (BTA — Bangsamoro Transition Authority) led by MILF
- First parliamentary elections: September 14, 2026 — the critical near-term milestone
- Government targets exit agreement by 2028 (before Marcos leaves office)
- **June 2026:** Comelec declares "very ready" — 6,500 automated counting machines, 2,393,530 registered voters, 5,212 clustered precincts across 1,186 voting centers. Gun ban July 16–Sept 29. ACM shading threshold proposed to increase from 15% to 25%.
- **May 2026:** BARMM placed under state of calamity (oil crisis + El Niño drought) — compounds pre-election instability.

## Risk factors
- **Spoiler groups:** MILF hardliners, Abu Sayyaf remnants (regaining ground in Sulu per Feb 2026 Patikul encounter), and IS-affiliated cells (Dawla Islamiyah emir killed April 2026) benefit from peace process failure
- **Manila interference:** Marcos administration's oversight posture risks alienating MILF leadership
- **Election violence:** September 2026 elections could trigger clan-based (rido) violence that extremists exploit — CCAA recorded 43 rido incidents in first 4 months of 2026
- **Resource control:** Disputes over Bangsamoro's fiscal autonomy and natural resource revenue sharing with Manila
- **MILF fragmentation:** BFP split from UBJP and Iqbal corruption crisis threaten MILF unity ahead of elections
- **Reintegration failures:** Three of those killed in the April 2026 Dawla Islamiyah operation were former surrenderees, exposing serious flaws in extremist reintegration programs

## Assessment
The peace process is at its most fragile point since 2019. CCAA warns violence will likely increase in 2026 as the region heads to its first parliamentary election. Violent extremism incidents rose from 29 (late 2025) to 40 (Jan–May 2026), with deaths nearly doubling. Successful elections would consolidate the MILF's political legitimacy and undermine extremist recruitment narratives. Failed or fraudulent elections could reverse years of progress.
