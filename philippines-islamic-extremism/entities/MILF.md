# Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF)

The largest and most politically sophisticated Moro insurgent group in the Philippines. Founded in 1984 as a splinter from the MNLF, the MILF sought an independent Islamic state in Mindanao before negotiating toward autonomy.

## Current status
- Signed the Comprehensive Agreement on the Bangsamoro (CAB) with the Philippine government in 2014
- Transitioned from armed movement to governing authority within BARMM
- First parliamentary elections scheduled for September 14, 2026 — a defining moment for the peace process
- Intra-MILF rivalries over electoral strategy, resource control, and relationships with Manila are intensifying
- In May 2026, forged a joint anti-ISIS front with the Philippine Army in Lanao del Norte

## Significance
The MILF's transformation from insurgent group to political authority is the central variable in the Mindanao peace process. If the September 2026 elections proceed credibly, the track record of Islamist governance in BARMM will influence whether splinter groups retain recruitment appeal. If elections are marred by fraud, violence, or Manila interference, spoiler factions could rearm.

## Key figures
- Murad Ebrahim — MILF Chairperson and BARMM Interim Chief Minister
- Mel Senen Sarmiento — Presidential Adviser on Peace, Reconciliation and Unity (appointed 2026)
