# China and Sri Lanka's Debt Crisis: Belt and Road Initiative Blowback
**Source:** Jamestown Foundation, China Brief, May 27, 2022
**Author:** Sudha Ramachandran

## Key Points

- Sri Lanka's usable forex reserves fell to just $50 million in early May 2022.
- Sri Lanka suspended repayment of $51 billion in debt owed to China, Japan, and other foreign creditors.
- Serious shortages of food, fuel, and medicine; inflation surged past 70%.
- Public anger targeted the Rajapaksa family, seen as corrupt and too close to China.

## Sino-Sri Lankan Relations

- Relations warmed considerably from 2005 under Mahinda Rajapaksa.
- China was among the strongest supporters of Sri Lanka's military campaign against the LTTE and provided weaponry.
- China shielded Sri Lanka from international pressure over alleged war crimes.
- During Mahinda's second term (2010-15), China massively expanded loans for infrastructure.
- Chinese submarines docked at Colombo harbor twice in 2014, despite India's objections.

## Post-2015 Period

- After Rajapaksa's 2015 defeat, the Sirisena-Wickremesinghe government promised "equal relations" with India and China.
- However, China's role did not diminish. Borrowing continued.
- The Wickremesinghe government's attempt to renegotiate Hambantota debt culminated in the 99-year lease to China Merchants Port.

## Return of the Rajapaksas (2019)

- Gotabaya Rajapaksa elected president in November 2019; Mahinda returned as PM.
- India-China contest for infrastructure: East Container Terminal deal (India/Japan) was scrapped and awarded to China Harbour Engineering Company.
- India recovered some ground with Trincomalee Oil Tank Farm agreement and West Container Terminal deal.

## China and the Economic Crisis

- China widely seen as responsible for running Sri Lanka into a debt trap.
- 10% of Sri Lanka's total foreign debt owed to China -- similar to Japan, far less than international sovereign bondholders and ADB.
- Chinese loans carried average 3.3% interest vs Japan's 0.7%; maturity 18 years vs 24 (India) and 34 (Japan).
- However, blaming China alone is flawed: most external debt is in ISBs and multilateral bank loans.
- An import-oriented economy dependent on tourism with little manufacturing was vulnerable to pandemic shocks.

## Future of Sri Lanka's Ties with China

- China's image and credibility in Sri Lanka severely damaged.
- Beijing seen as less generous than India during the crisis.
- Despite this, Chinese influence will continue because: (1) Rajapaksas may return, (2) IMF conditions may redirect anger away from China, (3) only China has deep pockets for infrastructure loans without political conditions.
- Any Sri Lankan leader will need to work with China as a critical development partner.

## Lessons for BRI Countries

- Bangladesh has been careful to maintain forex reserves.
- Nepal prefers grants over commercial loans.
- Other BRI countries are watching how China responds to Sri Lanka's crisis.
- China was initially reluctant to restructure Sri Lanka's debt.
- Sri Lanka's crisis will make BRI countries more cautious borrowers, but dependence on China will continue.
