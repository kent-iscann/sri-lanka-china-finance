# Timeline: Chinese Infrastructure Plays in Southern Africa

## 2019
- **Pre-2019:** China's presence in Southern African mining grows steadily through BRI-era infrastructure loans, with Chinese state-backed firms acquiring stakes in cobalt, copper, and lithium operations across the Copperbelt and Zimbabwe.

## 2022
- **Mid-2020s:** China controls an estimated 70% of DRC's mineral exports, with the majority routed through Dar es Salaam (Tanzania) and other Indian Ocean ports. Chinese firms dominate cobalt processing globally.

## 2023
- **2023:** Zimbabwe begins signaling intent to ban raw lithium concentrate exports, demanding that Chinese mining companies invest in domestic processing and beneficiation.

## 2024
- **2024:** Zimbabwe formally bans export of raw lithium concentrates and unbeneficiated minerals. Chinese firms begin committing capital for local processing plants.
- **2024:** China-backed Lobito Corridor project gains momentum — a planned 1,600km rail link from mineral-rich DRC/Zambia Copperbelt to the Atlantic port of Lobito in Angola, designed to bypass traditional South African and Tanzanian export routes.

## 2025
- **2025:** Chinese lithium miners (Sinomine Resource, Zhejiang Huayou Cobalt, Chengxin Lithium) invest ~$1 billion in lithium sulphate processing plants in Zimbabwe.
- **2025:** China becomes Africa's largest trading partner with bilateral trade exceeding $300 billion. Chinese firms operate 78 commercial ports across 32 African countries.
- **2025:** US-China competition for African critical minerals intensifies. US secures Greenland rare earth deal (Tanbreez project) partly to reduce dependence on African/Chinese supply chains.

## 2026
- **April 2026:** Zimbabwe exports its first shipment of lithium sulphate from the Arcadia mine near Harare — the first such product from both Zimbabwe and the African continent.
- **April 2026:** Two Chinese firms (Chengxin Lithium, Sinomine Resource) granted lithium export quotas by Zimbabwe.
- **May 2026:** Zimbabwe's Mines Secretary publicly demands Chinese investors build beneficiation plants domestically — lithium processing, platinum smelting, diamond polishing.
- **May 2026:** Chatham House report notes China's BRI infrastructure (Lobito Corridor, TAZARA railway) has created entrenched critical mineral supply chains that Western nations cannot easily replicate.
- **May 2026:** Chinese firm confirms rare earth discovery (neodymium, praseodymium) in Tanzania's Njombe Region.
- **June 2026:** Flooding in western Angola suspends rail traffic along the Lobito Corridor, disrupting a key export route for critical minerals from DRC/Zambia.
- **June 2026:** EU launches first-ever investment roadshow in South Africa, announcing €12B package under the 2025 EU-South Africa Clean Trade and Investment Partnership. South Africa demands beneficiation as condition for mineral access.
- **June 2026:** Chinese company Hongji Mining Co. Ltd partners with Tanzanian government for rare earth exploration at Mkiu Village, Ludewa District, Njombe Region. Tanzania confirmed as holder of graphite, nickel, helium, and rare earth reserves.
