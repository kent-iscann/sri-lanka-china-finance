# Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC)

The DRC is the epicenter of China's Southern African mineral strategy. The country holds the world's largest cobalt reserves (roughly 70% of global supply) and significant copper, coltan, and lithium deposits. Chinese firms — primarily through the CMOC Group, China Molybdenum, and various state-backed entities — control an estimated 70% of the DRC's mineral exports. The vast majority of these exports are routed to China for processing. China's infrastructure investments in the DRC (roads, railways, ports) are explicitly tied to mining concessions under the "infrastructure-for-resources" model. The DRC is also a key node in the planned Lobito Corridor, which would give China-backed operations an Atlantic export route.

## Key minerals
- Cobalt (world's largest reserves)
- Copper
- Coltan (tantalum)
- Lithium

## Key Chinese actors
- CMOC Group (China Molybdenum)
- Zhejiang Huayou Cobalt
- Sinomine Resource Group
