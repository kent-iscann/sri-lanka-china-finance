# Angola

Angola is a critical infrastructure node in China's Southern African mineral strategy. The Lobito Corridor — a planned 1,600km railway from the Zambian/DRC Copperbelt to the Atlantic port of Lobito — would give Chinese-backed mining operations an alternative export route that bypasses both South African ports and the Indian Ocean route through Dar es Salaam. Angola also has significant diamond and iron ore deposits of interest to Chinese firms. The country has a long history of oil-for-infrastructure deals with China dating back to the 2000s. However, the Lobito Corridor has faced delays, including flooding in western Angola in mid-2026 that disrupted rail traffic.

## Key minerals
- Diamonds
- Iron ore
- Oil (strategic, not mineral per se)

## Key role
- Atlantic export gateway via Lobito Corridor
- Historical oil-for-infrastructure relationship with China
