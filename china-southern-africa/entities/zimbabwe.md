# Zimbabwe

Zimbabwe holds Africa's largest lithium reserves and significant deposits of platinum, gold, chrome, and diamonds. In 2024, the government banned the export of raw lithium concentrates — a move specifically designed to force Chinese mining companies to invest in domestic processing and beneficiation. The policy worked: Chinese firms (Zhejiang Huayou Cobalt, Sinomine Resource, Chengxin Lithium) committed ~$1 billion to lithium sulphate processing plants. In April 2026, Zimbabwe exported its first shipment of lithium sulphate from the Arcadia mine — a continental first. The government has also demanded Chinese investors build platinum smelters and diamond polishing centers locally. Zimbabwe represents a case study in using resource nationalism to shift China's role from raw extractor to domestic processor.
