# Zambia

Zambia sits at the heart of the Copperbelt, one of the world's richest copper and cobalt producing regions. ~40% of Zambia's mineral exports are routed through Tanzanian ports (primarily Dar es Salaam), with the remainder flowing through South African ports and, increasingly, the planned Lobito Corridor to Angola's Atlantic coast. China is Zambia's largest bilateral creditor and has invested heavily in copper mining operations and associated infrastructure. The TAZARA railway (Tanzania-Zambia Railway Authority), originally built by China in the 1970s and recently rehabilitated with Chinese backing, is a critical mineral export artery. Zambia is also a partner in the Lobito Corridor project, which would create a transcontinental route from the Copperbelt to the Atlantic.

## Key minerals
- Copper
- Cobalt
- Uranium

## Key Chinese actors
- China Nonferrous Metal Mining (CNMC)
- Various BRI infrastructure financiers
- TAZARA (joint Tanzania-Zambia railway with Chinese backing)
