# Tanzania

Tanzania serves as a key Indian Ocean export gateway for landlocked mineral-rich nations (DRC, Zambia, Zimbabwe). Approximately 70% of DRC's exports to China pass through the Port of Dar es Salaam. The TAZARA railway (1,860km, Zambia to Dar es Salaam), originally built by China in the 1970s and recently rehabilitated, is a critical mineral logistics artery. Tanzania is also emerging as a rare earth producer — in May 2026, a Chinese firm confirmed significant neodymium and praseodymium deposits in the Njombe Region. Tanzania has also signed agreements linking its ports to the Lobito Corridor vision, creating transcontinental Atlantic-Indian Ocean mineral routes.

## Key role
- Indian Ocean export gateway (Port of Dar es Salaam)
- TAZARA railway terminus
- Emerging rare earth producer

## Key minerals
- Neodymium, praseodymium (rare earths, newly confirmed)
- Gold
- Tanzanite
