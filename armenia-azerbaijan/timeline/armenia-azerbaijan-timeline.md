# Timeline: Armenia-Azerbaijan Relations

## 2020
- **September:** Second Nagorno-Karabakh War begins. Azerbaijan, backed by Turkey, launches major offensive against Armenian forces in the disputed territory.
- **November 10:** Russia-brokered ceasefire ends the 44-day war. Azerbaijan regains significant territory. Russian peacekeepers deployed to Nagorno-Karabakh.

## 2021
- **April:** Tensions rise as Azerbaijan detains Armenian soldiers near the border, violating the ceasefire.
- **November:** Clashes along the Armenia-Azerbaijan border kill dozens on both sides.

## 2022
- **September:** Azerbaijan launches major offensive along the Armenia-Azerbaijan border, seizing strategic positions inside internationally recognized Armenian territory.
- **October:** Pashinyan signals willingness to recognize Nagorno-Karabakh as part of Azerbaijan in exchange for peace guarantees.

## 2023
- **September:** Azerbaijan launches 24-hour military operation in Nagorno-Karabakh, dissolving the self-declared republic. Over 100,000 ethnic Armenians flee the region.
- **December:** Armenia and Azerbaijan agree to exchange prisoners and begin peace talks.

## 2024
- **May:** Armenia returns four border villages to Azerbaijan as a goodwill gesture.
- **July:** Turkey and Armenia resume diplomatic normalization talks.
- **August:** US-brokered negotiations on the TRUMP Route for International Peace and Prosperity (TRIPP) corridor begin.

## 2025
- **August:** Trump brokers Armenia-Azerbaijan peace framework at White House summit. Both sides agree to pursue a comprehensive peace treaty.
- **Fall:** Russia's relations with Armenia deteriorate sharply. Armenia hosts EU and Zelenskyy for European Political Community summit. Putin warns of "Ukrainian scenario."

## 2026
- **May 11:** Turkey lifts restrictions on direct trade declarations with Armenia after 33 years.
- **May 26:** US Secretary of State Marco Rubio visits Yerevan, signs minerals deal and TRIPP corridor agreement.
- **June 3:** Historic Armenia-Turkey business forum held in Kars.
- **June 7:** Armenia holds parliamentary elections. Pashinyan's Civil Contract party wins 49.8% of votes, securing a majority. International observers document Russian interference.
- **June 8:** Pashinyan declares victory, mandates peace talks with Azerbaijan and continued Western pivot.
