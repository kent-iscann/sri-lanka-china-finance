# Donald Trump

**Role:** President of the United States (since 2025, second term)

## Relevance to Armenia-Azerbaijan
Trump brokered the August 2025 Armenia-Azerbaijan peace framework at a White House summit. His administration has pursued an active diplomatic role in the South Caucasus, including the TRUMP Route for International Peace and Prosperity (TRIPP) corridor.

## Key Actions
- **August 2025:** Hosted Armenia-Azerbaijan peace summit at the White House
- **May 2026:** Secretary of State Marco Rubio visited Yerevan, signed minerals deal and TRIPP corridor agreement
- **Ongoing:** US has offered to manage the TRIPP transport corridor through southern Armenia

## Unpredictability Factor
The Trump administration's approach to the region is transactional and can shift abruptly. Key risks:
- Trump's focus may shift to other priorities, leaving the peace process without its most powerful external backer
- The administration's willingness to commit US resources (including potentially security personnel) to the TRIPP corridor is untested
- Relations with Russia and Turkey may take precedence over Armenia-Azerbaijan peace at any given moment
- The administration's domestic political calculations may override foreign policy commitments

## Strategic Interest
The US interest in the South Caucasus is driven by:
- Countering Russian influence in the region
- Securing critical minerals partnerships (Rubio signed a minerals deal with Armenia)
- Establishing the TRIPP corridor as a viable alternative to Russian-controlled transit routes
