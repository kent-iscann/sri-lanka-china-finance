# Ilham Aliyev

**Role:** President of Azerbaijan (since 2003)

## Profile
Aliyev has ruled Azerbaijan since succeeding his father in 2003. He has consolidated power through a combination of hydrocarbon wealth, military success, and authoritarian governance. Azerbaijan's victory in the 2020 Nagorno-Karabakh war and the 2023 dissolution of the self-declared republic dramatically strengthened his domestic position.

## Key Positions
- **Nagorno-Karabakh:** Considers the issue resolved following the 2023 military operation. Over 100,000 ethnic Armenians fled the region.
- **Peace Treaty:** Has committed to a comprehensive peace treaty with Armenia but insists on terms that reflect Azerbaijan's military victory, including transit rights through southern Armenia (Zangezur corridor).
- **Turkey Alliance:** Maintains a close strategic partnership with Turkey, which was instrumental in Azerbaijan's 2020 military victory.
- **Balancing Act:** Has cautiously welcomed the US-brokered TRIPP corridor while maintaining relations with Russia.

## Military Position
Azerbaijan's military is significantly more powerful than Armenia's, a gap that widened after the 2020 war. Azerbaijan controls strategic positions inside internationally recognized Armenian territory along the border. This military superiority gives Aliyev significant leverage in peace negotiations.

## Key Relationships
- **Nikol Pashinyan (Armenia):** Negotiating partner. Aliyev holds the stronger hand militarily and territorially.
- **Recep Tayyip Erdoğan (Turkey):** Close strategic ally. The Turkey-Azerbaijan axis is a cornerstone of Baku's foreign policy.
- **Vladimir Putin (Russia):** Relations are pragmatic. Russia is Azerbaijan's largest arms supplier, but Aliyev has diversified partnerships.
- **Donald Trump (US):** Engaged through the TRIPP corridor framework. Aliyev cautiously welcomed the US-brokered deal.
