# Zangezur Corridor

## Overview
A proposed transit route through Armenia's Syunik (Zangezur) province connecting mainland Azerbaijan to its Nakhchivan exclave. Azerbaijan has consistently pushed for this corridor as a condition of peace, framing it as analogous to the Lachin corridor that connected Armenia to Nagorno-Karabakh.

## Azerbaijan's Position
Aliyev considers the Zangezur corridor a non-negotiable element of any peace agreement. Azerbaijan argues it needs unimpeded transit rights through southern Armenia, with or without Armenian customs controls.

## Armenia's Position
Pashinyan has resisted the term "Zangezur corridor" and any arrangement that would compromise Armenian sovereignty over the route. Armenia has proposed opening roads and railways under Armenian sovereignty and customs control, but Azerbaijan has rejected this.

## US/TRIPP Dimension
The Trump-brokered TRIPP corridor addresses a similar geographic reality but frames it as a US-backed economic corridor rather than an Azerbaijani demand. This reframing may provide Pashinyan political cover to implement what is functionally a transit route through Syunik while maintaining the fiction of Armenian sovereignty.

## Strategic Implications
- If implemented with US security personnel, it would establish a permanent American presence in the South Caucasus
- Russia views this as a direct threat to its influence in the region
- Iran views any corridor along its border with Armenia as a potential security threat
