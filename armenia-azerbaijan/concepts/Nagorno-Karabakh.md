# Nagorno-Karabakh

## Overview
A mountainous region in the South Caucasus that was internationally recognized as part of Azerbaijan but had a majority ethnic Armenian population. The dispute over Nagorno-Karabakh was the core of the Armenia-Azerbaijan conflict for decades.

## History
- **1923:** Soviet authorities placed Nagorno-Karabakh under Azerbaijani administration as an autonomous oblast.
- **1988:** The regional Soviet voted to join Armenia, triggering ethnic conflict.
- **1991-1994:** First Nagorno-Karabakh War. Armenian forces, supported by Armenia, took control of the region and seven surrounding Azerbaijani districts.
- **1994-2020:** Frozen conflict. The self-declared Nagorno-Karabakh Republic operated with Armenian support but was not internationally recognized.
- **2020:** Second Nagorno-Karabakh War. Azerbaijan, backed by Turkey and armed with advanced drones, recaptured significant territory.
- **September 2023:** Azerbaijan launched a 24-hour military operation, dissolving the self-declared republic. Over 100,000 ethnic Armenians fled to Armenia.

## Current Status
Nagorno-Karabakh is now fully under Azerbaijani control. The Armenian population has been almost entirely displaced. Azerbaijan has begun reconstruction and resettlement programs. The issue is considered resolved by Azerbaijan but remains a source of deep trauma and political controversy in Armenia.

## Relevance to Peace Process
Pashinyan's willingness to formally recognize Azerbaijani sovereignty over Nagorno-Karabakh was a prerequisite for peace talks. This recognition is deeply unpopular in Armenia and was a major issue in the 2026 parliamentary elections.
