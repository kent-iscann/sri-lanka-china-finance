# Regime Survival Doctrine

Georgian Dream's foreign policy since 2022 can best be understood as a "regime survival doctrine" — prioritizing the party's hold on power over any consistent ideological or strategic orientation. This explains Georgia's seemingly contradictory moves: seeking a US reset while cozying up to Moscow, suspending EU talks while claiming to want eventual membership, cracking down on protesters while making belated arrests of police officers involved in violence.

## Key principles
1. **Avoid becoming Ukraine:** GD's core fear is that too-close alignment with the West could provoke Russian military intervention
2. **Transactional engagement:** Engage with all powers (US, EU, Russia, China, Turkey) based on immediate regime needs, not long-term strategy
3. **Domestic control:** Foreign policy serves domestic narrative — "protecting Georgia from war" while suppressing dissent
4. **Ivanishvili's calculus:** The de facto ruler personally drives major foreign policy shifts; his business interests and relationships (including with Trump) shape Georgia's orientation

## Implications
- Georgia's foreign policy is highly personalist and can shift suddenly based on Ivanishvili's calculations
- Long-term institutional commitments (EU, NATO) are subordinated to short-term regime needs
- The "multi-vector" approach risks leaving Georgia without a firm anchor in any camp
- Western institutions view GD as unreliable; Russia views Georgia as too independent to fully trust
