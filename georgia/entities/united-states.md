# United States

The US-Georgia relationship has undergone a dramatic transformation. Once considered Georgia's most important strategic partner (Georgia was a top per-capita contributor to US-led missions in Iraq and Afghanistan), the relationship deteriorated sharply after 2024.

## Timeline of deterioration
- **2024:** US suspends strategic partnership after Georgia passes foreign agents law
- **2025:** Relations remain frozen; US strengthens ties with Armenia instead
- **March 2026:** Secretary Rubio calls PM Kobakhidze — first high-level contact
- **April 2026:** Trump Tower Tbilisi announced
- **May 2026:** Deputy Assistant Secretary Sonata Coulter visits Tbilisi; second US State Department delegation (Yockey/Andreoli) follows later in May
- **June 8, 2026:** US House passes HR 7668 "Countering China's Control of the Caucasus Act" with bipartisan support
- **June 10, 2026:** Rubio expresses "serious concerns" about Chinese influence in Georgia

## Current state
The Trump administration appears open to a "reset" with Georgia, potentially driven by:
1. **Middle Corridor interest:** Georgia's role as a China-Europe transport route aligns with US interest in diversifying supply chains away from Russia and Iran
2. **Transactional approach:** Trump's personal/business engagement style (Trump Tower Tbilisi) may bypass traditional diplomatic friction
3. **Armenia competition:** US signing strategic agreements with Armenia may be leverage to pull Georgia back toward Western orbit

## Congressional pushback
Despite executive branch openness, Congress has grown increasingly alarmed:
- **HR 7668 (June 2026):** Bipartisan bill mandating classified report on Russian/Chinese intelligence assets in Georgia and a 5-year US-Georgia strategy. Cites Anaklia deep-water port (Chinese consortium selected over US company) as key concern.
- **MEGOBARI Act:** Previously passed House legislation to strengthen support for Georgian democratic institutions and civil society
- **Joe Wilson (R-SC):** Called GD "illegitimate regime" selling out to CCP, Putin, and Iran
- **Brian Mast (R-FL):** Cited Anaklia port as critical infrastructure risk from PRC
- **Ami Bera (D-CA):** Linked bill to democratic backsliding, said GD doesn't represent Georgian people

## Key uncertainty
Whether the US reset is durable or merely a transactional pause. Congress is moving toward conditionality (intelligence assessment, strategy review) while the executive branch pursues engagement. Kobakhidze called bill sponsor Wilson "absolutely frivolous" but says he's having "specific conversations" with the Trump administration. The EU remains deeply skeptical of Georgian Dream.
