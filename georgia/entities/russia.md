# Russia

Russia remains the most consequential external actor in Georgia's geopolitical positioning. Moscow occupies approximately 20% of Georgian territory (Abkhazia and South Ossetia) since the 2008 war and maintains significant military presence there. Russia's invasion of Ukraine in 2022 paradoxically created both pressure and opportunity for Georgia: pressure because it demonstrated Russia's willingness to use military force against neighbors seeking Western integration, and opportunity because Russia's international isolation made it less able to punish Georgia for drifting away from the West.

## Current dynamics
- **Occupation:** Russian forces in Abkhazia and South Ossetia; creeping borderization
- **Economic leverage:** Russia is a significant trade partner and remittance source; Georgian economy benefits from Russian tourism and money flows post-2022
- **Coercion mix:** Economic pressure, political influence operations, military presence on occupied territories
- **2026:** Relations between Georgian Dream and Moscow are closer than at any point since 2008, though Georgia has not formally recognized the occupied territories or aligned with Russian foreign policy

## Key question
Whether Georgia's current "pragmatic" engagement with Russia is a temporary survival strategy or a permanent reorientation.
