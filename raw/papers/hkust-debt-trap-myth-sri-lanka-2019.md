# The "Chinese Debt Trap" and its Sri Lanka Example
**Source:** HKUST Institute for Emerging Market Studies, Thought Leadership Brief No. 29, September 2019
**Authors:** Barry Sautman (HKUST) and Yan Hairong (Hong Kong Polytechnic University)

## Key Arguments

- The Chinese Debt Trap (CDT) is a conspiracy theory reflecting US-led counter-mobilization against China's Belt and Road Initiative.
- No CDT exists anywhere, including in Sri Lanka, the case most ubiquitously cited by media.
- Hambantota International Port (HIP) is NOT part of a debt trap, NOT a white elephant, NOT a debt-for-equity swap, and NOT a violation of sovereignty.

## Evidence Presented

- Sri Lankans had discussed building a port at Hambantota since the 1920s.
- In 2006-2007, Sri Lanka asked India and then Japan to finance Hambantota Port. Both declined.
- The Sri Lankan government had to actively persuade China to finance and build HIP from 2007-2012.
- In 2016, the government decided to lease out HIP and again approached India and Japan, who again declined. The government then lobbied hard to attract a Chinese firm.
- The lease did NOT result from pressing debt to China. By 2016, only 9% of government external debt or 15% of all public entity external debt was owed to China.
- The SLPA could use profits from Colombo Port to make HIP loan payments (~$67m/year in 2016-2017).
- Meanwhile, the government needed billions to repay International Sovereign Bonds (ISBs) and commercial bank loans held by Western investors.
- About two-thirds of Chinese loans are concessional, averaging 3.2% interest, repayable over 20 years.
- HIP's lease is NOT a debt-for-equity swap: CMPort put up $1.12 billion, more than HIP's valuation, for less than 70% share.
- 99-year leases are not uncommon: Australian states have leased half a dozen major ports for 99 years to entities of varied nationality.
- No national asset anywhere outside China has been forfeited to Chinese lenders.
- China has restructured loans for scores of debtor states rather than pressing countries that have difficulties.

## Political Context

- The CDT notion is embedded in racist ideology (Yellow Perilism) and is a political instrument.
- US elites (Pence, Pompeo) and allies (India, Japan, Australia) aggressively promote the CDT narrative.
- Opposition forces in BRI countries invoke CDT to discredit ruling parties that seek Chinese loans.
